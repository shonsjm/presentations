## Background

---
# We can know about First stars from ultra metal-poor stars
- First stars formed from primordial gas, mostly H and He.
- Second-gen stars formed from mixture of ejecta of First SNe explosion and primordial gas. ([Fe/H]<-3.0)
  -  Especially, ultra-metal poor (UMP) stars with $[\mathrm{Fe/H}] <−4.0$ are likely formed from gas enriched by individual first-SNe events (Frebel+ 2015; Hartwig+ 2018)
- Intersteller medium became homogeneous after cycles of those. (-3.0<[Fe/H]<-2.5)(Argast+ 2000; Tumlinson 2006)

→ **Evidence on the first stars/SNe can be obtained from the chemical signatures of surviving low-mass, UMP stars with $[\mathrm{Fe/H}] <−4.0$ (Beers & Christlieb 2005)**

## Previous work

# Difficulty of spherical explosion models
![bg right:50% fit](https://www.evernote.com/l/AQa9mAOrlQtBfahBX5o1lNQsZ-5kydRam0EB/image.png)
<!-- _footer: Fig: II型超新星 - Wikipedia -->
Spherical explosion model cannot produce those 3 characteristics at the same time
- Small energy:
  - cannot produce $[\mathrm{Co, Zn/Fe}] > 0$
- Large energy:
  - produce $[\mathrm{Co, Zn/Fe}] > 0$ (Nomoto+ 2013)
  - but high $[\mathrm{Fe/H}]$

(Cayrel+ 2004, Woosley+ 1995)

→ **Needs aspherical effects?**

---
# Mixing & Fallback Model was developed
![bg right:50% fit vertical](https://www.evernote.com/l/AQYgff7VgalLc7lyvlAtoKhN0XciLLPA848B/image.png)
![bg right:50% fit](https://www.evernote.com/l/AQYUd16x1utIsbb2Psu3-5xfvZC1d8re4jMB/image.png)
<!-- _footer: Fig: Tominaga+ 2007 -->

“mixing and fallback” (MF) SNe model was developed (Umeda & Nomoto 2002) and it can mimic 2 different mechanisms:

- (a) Faint quasi-spherical MF SNe
  - Rayleigh–Taylor instability (Joggerst+ 2009, 2010)
- (b) Aspherical bipolar jet SNe
  - Jet-like explosion (Tominaga+ 2007, Tominaga 2009)

---

## This work

---
# How about HMP star with [Fe /H]<-5.0?
![bg right:50% fit](https://www.evernote.com/l/AQbVLGfaKEpLso78POiABF6ApT9h_ZAIw_MB/image.png)
<!-- _footer: Fig: Tominaga+ 2007 -->

- They determine $[\mathrm{Zn} / \mathrm{Fe}]=0.80 \pm 0.25$ of hyper metal-poor (HMP) star, HE1327--2326, with $[\mathrm{Fe} / \mathrm{H}]=-5.2$ for the first time
- The goal of this work is interpretation of new zinc abundance on such a metal-poor star

---
# Faint, quasi-spherical model 
![bg right:50% fit](https://www.evernote.com/l/AQZBdAECWzdI5YxDiouptrkZNykyuY3-FEgB/image.png)
<!-- _footer: Fig: Ezzeddine+ 2019 -->
- Performed statistical fitting tests following Placco+ 2016
  - Grid has 16,800 models
    - mass: $10$ -- $100 M_\odot$
    - energies: ($0.3$ -- $10) \times 10^{51}$ erg

→ $27 M_\odot$ progenitor with $0.3 \times 10^{51}$ erg explosion's yield is best fit(82%)
→ **None of them matches high [Zn/Fe] abundance**

---
# Aspherical model

![bg right:50% fit](https://www.evernote.com/l/AQajKYD4cGZKNbapDNtgauWlisvOav5HZVgB/image.png)
<!-- _footer: Fig: Ezzeddine+ 2019 -->

- Tominaga+ 2007
  - mass:$20$ -- $50 M_\odot$ 
  - energy:($5$ -- $40) \times 10^{51}$ erg
  - density factor: 1/4 -- 1/2
- **Density modified MF model mimicking an aspherical explosion with bipolar-outflows in certain range of mass and higher energy**
- The yield of $25 M_\odot$ progenitor's exploding with $E = 5 \times 10^{51} \mathrm{ergs}$ is best match

---
# Result
- Determine the abundance of $\mathrm{Zn}$ on HMP ($[\mathrm{Fe/H}] = -5.2$) star for the first time
- Found the yields of density-modified MF model mimicking aspherical SN explosion with bipolar outflow matches better than other models

---
# Interpretation

- A high-velocity ejecta could facilitate carrying the SNe yields out of the parent host minihalo to enrich a neighboring minihalo.
